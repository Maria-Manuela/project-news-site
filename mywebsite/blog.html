Confessions of an Innocent Plant Serial Killer

Let’s not beat around the bush anymore. You walked into that nursery looking fresh as a daisy, full of hope and wearing your best gardening apron. You looked at that dramatic Fiddle-Leaf Fig and thought, "We are going to be best friends."

Spoiler alert: you were barking up the wrong tree.

Within three weeks, that poor plant was dropping leaves faster than gossip on a grapevine. You panicked. You tried to nip the problem in the bud by drowning it in four gallons of water, effectively giving it webbed feet. It died anyway.

Now you are standing over a pot of crunchy brown twigs, wondering where it all went wrong. Take a deep breath. It is time to turn over a new leaf, accept your dark past, and go buy a plastic cactus.
