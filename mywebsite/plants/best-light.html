Lighting 101: How to Get Jungle Vibes Without Murdering Your Plants

So, you want your apartment to look like a lush Jumanji jungle? That is a beautiful dream. But let's not beat around the bush—if you put your new tropical monsters in a dark closet, your jungle ambitions are going to kick the bucket real fast.

Getting the right light isn't rocket science, but if you just guess, you are definitely barked up the wrong tree. Here is how to chase the light like a pro:

  • The Bright, Direct Sun (The Scorcher) This is the window where the sun hits like a laser beam. Put a delicate fern here, and it will be burnt to a crisp before you can say "fresh as a daisy." Save this spot for cacti who love the heat.

  • The Bright, Indirect Light (The Sweet Spot) This is the holy grail for jungle vibes. Think of it as a sunny room, but the plant is sitting a few feet back from the window. Your Monstera and Pothos will grow faster than gossip on a grapevine here.

  • The Low Light (The Dungeon) Got a room that looks like a vampire's cave? Don’t panic and start clutching at straws trying to grow a fiddle-leaf fig there. Stick to a Snake Plant or a ZZ plant. They don't mind the gloom.

Stop keeping your plants in the dark! Find the right window, and your indoor jungle will come up smelling of roses in no time.
