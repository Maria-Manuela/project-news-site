Is Your Plant Thirsty, or Are You Just Trying to Drown It?

Let’s be completely honest: love can be suffocating. Especially when that love comes out of a two-liter watering can every single day. You thought you were being a great plant parent, but let's not beat around the bush—your plant is currently wearing a life jacket.

If your plant is wilting, don’t blame it on the heat. It’s not the sun’s fault; it’s your fault. You’ve given it a full-body massage with the hose, and it’s not a pleasant experience. Your plant is begging for mercy, but you’re not listening. not listening.

When the soil looks like a swamp and smells like a wet dog, it is time to hit the panic button. Stop clutching at straws thinking that "just one more drop" will fix it. Your plant doesn't need a drink; it needs a lifeguard. Put the watering can down, step away from the pot, and let that poor thing breathe before it completely goes under!

How to Tell If You Overwatered:

The Setup: You wanted to show your plant some extra love.

The Punchline: But you really stepped on a banana skin here.

The Reality: Your plant is not thirsty; it is literally drowning.

The Plant Truth: It is about to kick the bucket from your kindness.

The Drama: The roots are rotting, but you are still clutching at straws.

The Sign: Yellow leaves mean it is time to hit the panic button.

The Verdict: Stop watering before your living room turns into a swamp.
