Repotting: How to Change Your Plant’s Outfit Without Killing It

Let’s not beat around the bush—your plant has outgrown its home. Its roots are busting out of the bottom like muffin-top jeans, and it is practically begging for a upgrade. But before you grab a massive bucket and some random dirt from the garden, stop. You might be barking up the wrong tree.

Repotting is the ultimate trust exercise between you and your green victim. Do it wrong, and your plant will kick the bucket from pure shock before Tuesday.

Here is your survival guide to changing pots:

  • The Size Matters Rule. Don't go from a tiny teacup to a giant bucket. Your plant will drown in all that extra wet soil, and you'll be clutching at straws trying to save it from root rot. Just go up one size!

  • The Dirt Gossip. Standard backyard dirt is too heavy. Your indoor plants want that fluffy, premium potting mix. Use the right soil, and your plant will grow faster than gossip on a grapevine.

  • The Post-Op Drama. After repotting, your plant might droop and look dead for a week. Don't hit the panic button! It is just throwing a tantrum. Give it some time, and it will come up smelling of roses.

Roll up your sleeves, get soil all over the living room floor, and give your plant a new set of clothes!
